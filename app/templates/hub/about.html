About KS1 Learning Hub 🎈

Little games for big learning — made for children aged 4–7.

What is this website?

KS1 Learning Hub is a collection of free, friendly learning games for children in Key Stage 1 (Reception, Years 1 and 2). Every game is built around things children are learning at school in England — phonics, spelling, reading, counting, money and telling the time — and wrapped up in bright colours, big buttons and plenty of encouraging comments.

Our aim 🌟

Practice makes progress — but worksheets can feel like a chore. The aim of this hub is simple: make practising feel like playing. Short games, instant feedback, and no pressure. Children can try, get it wrong, and try again — mistakes here cost nothing but earn stars when they're fixed.

Everything is designed for small hands and growing readers: chunky tappable buttons, simple instructions, and friendly sounds and characters instead of timers and test scores.

What can children learn?

📚 English

  • 🔍 Story Detective — reading comprehension: read a short story and work out which words are missing from the clues in the text.
  • 🧙 Word Wizard — listen-and-spell: hear a word, then type or build it, stage by stage.
  • 🔤 Letter Quest — a child-sized crossword for spelling and vocabulary.
  • 🔊 Sound Sort — phonics: choose the right sound to complete the word.
  • 🃏 Match Pairs — flip the cards to match pictures and words, building vocabulary and memory.

🔢 Maths

  • ➕ Maths Drill — quick-fire sums to build number confidence.
  • 💰 Coin Counter — recognising UK coins and making amounts.
  • 🕐 Clock Master — telling the time on an analogue clock.
  • 🧩 Sudoku — mini picture-sudoku puzzles for logic and careful thinking.

How to get around 🗺️

  • Pick a game from the home page cards, or use the menus in the top bar — they work from any page.
  • Choose a level. Most games have a Year 1 / Year 2 or difficulty switch near the top. Year 1 is gentler; Year 2 adds more of a challenge.
  • Play! Big coloured buttons do the work — tap words, coins, letters or numbers. The progress bar shows how far through you are.
  • Check and try again. Buttons like Check Answers and Try Again mean nothing is ever locked in — wrong answers just hop back out for another go.
  • The logo in the top corner always takes you back home.

A note for grown-ups 🧑‍🏫

The games are inspired by the KS1 curriculum in England, and they work best as little-and-often practice — five or ten minutes at a time — alongside what children are doing at school. They play nicely on tablets, laptops and interactive whiteboards.
